EEG

Electroencephalography

Using EEG to capture real-time brain activity and understand cognitive processes in human-automation interaction scenarios, providing insights into attention, trust calibration, and cognitive load.

Research Focus

EEG has emerged as a primary method for studying cognitive processes in human-AI interaction (HAII). Its increasing use, particularly in assessing how users respond to AI recommendations and handle uncertainty, has provided valuable insights into attention shifts, cognitive load, and real-time trust calibration.

This approach enables a deeper understanding of cognitive dynamics during human-automation interaction, offering a foundation for designing systems that better align with human cognitive processes and enhance the overall effectiveness of human-machine collaboration.

EEG Applications & Research Directions

EEG Applications in Human-Automation Interaction

Research Applications:

EEG technology is utilized to monitor and analyze brain activity patterns during various human-automation interaction scenarios, providing real-time insights into cognitive states and decision-making processes.

Key Measurement Areas:

  • Attention shifts and focus allocation during automated task performance
  • Cognitive load assessment in complex multi-task environments
  • Real-time trust calibration and confidence levels
  • Neural responses to uncertainty and decision-making under automation
